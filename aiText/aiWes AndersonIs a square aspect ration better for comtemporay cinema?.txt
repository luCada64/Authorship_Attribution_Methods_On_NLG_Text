Title: Is a square aspect ratio better for contemporary cinema?

Author: Wes Anderson

Year: 2016

Abstract:
This paper explores the use of a square aspect ratio in contemporary cinema from a stylized and visually innovative perspective. Drawing inspiration from classic films and art history, this study considers the aesthetic impact and storytelling possibilities of utilizing a square format in filmmaking. Through a series of case studies and analysis of visual techniques, this research discusses the potential benefits and challenges of breaking away from traditional widescreen formats in order to create a more immersive and thought-provoking cinematic experience. Ultimately, this paper argues that a square aspect ratio offers a unique and compelling visual language that can enhance the storytelling power of cinema in the modern age.
